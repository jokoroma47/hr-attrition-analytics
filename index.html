Top 5 retention recommendations
1
Cap projects at 5 maximum
6+ projects triggers 55%+ attrition. 7 projects = 100% attrition with zero exceptions. The optimal workload is 3–4 projects at under 10%.
2
Review the low-salary band urgently
Low salary drives 29.7% attrition vs 6.6% for high — a 4.5x multiplier. Nearly half the workforce (48.8%) sits in this band.
3
Increase the promotion rate from 2.1%
Promoted staff leave at 6% vs 24.2% for unpromoted — a 4x protective effect. The current 2.1% rate is critically low.
4
Intervene at Year 3–5 tenure
56.6% of 5-year employees leave. Engagement programmes targeting Year 3 employees could prevent the Year 5 exodus before it happens.
5
Audit the HR department culture
HR has the highest attrition at 29.1% — the team responsible for retention cannot retain itself. A systemic culture review is urgently needed.
